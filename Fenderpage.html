FENDER STRATOCASTER

The Fender Stratocaster, colloquially known as the Strat, is a model of double-cutaway electric guitar designed between 1952 and 1954 by Leo Fender, Bill Carson, George Fullerton, and Freddie Tavares.

The Fender Musical Instruments Corporation has continuously manufactured the Stratocaster since 1954.

Common features of the Strat include: The iconic tremolo system, the bright punchy sound of the single coil pickups and of course the most iconic guitar shape of all time

Famous guitarists who used the strat include: Eric Clapton, Jimi Hendrix, Jeff Beck and Stevie Ray Vaughan.

FENDER TELECASTER

The Fender Telecaster, colloquially known as the Tele, is an electric guitar produced by Fender.

Together with its sister model the Esquire, it was the world's first mass-produced, commercially successful solid-body electric guitar.

Its simple yet effective design and revolutionary sound broke ground and set trends in electric guitar manufacturing and popular music.

Fanous players include: Bruce Springsteen, Prince and Keith Richards.

FENDER JAGUAR

The Fender Jaguar is an electric guitar by Fender Musical Instruments characterized by an offset-waist body, a relatively unusual switching system with two separate circuits for lead and rhythm, and a short-scale 24" neck.

Owing some roots to the Jazzmaster, it was introduced in 1962 as Fender's feature-laden top-of-the-line model, designed to lure players from Gibson.

Famous players include: Kurt Cobain, Johnny Marr and Josh Klinghoffer.
